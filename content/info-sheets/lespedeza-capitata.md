## _Lespedeza capitata_

**Native to:** All of New England

**Life cycle:** Perennial

**Light**: Full/Partial Sun

**Soil Moisture:** Medium to Dry

**Height**: 2-4’<br />**Spacing**: 1-2’

**Blooms**: August-September<br />
**Bloom Color:** Green, Cream

**Natural Habitat:** Human-disturbed or -maintained habitats, grassland, meadows and fields, woodlands

**Benefits**: Pollinator Favorite, Bird Favorite, Host Plant (eg. Cloudywings, Hoary Edge, Eastern Tailed Blue)

> “Round Headed Bush Clover is a host plant to a number of butterflies and moths, including the Southern Cloudywing Butterfly, Northern Cloudywing Butterfly, Hoary Edge Butterfly, the Eastern Tailed-Blue, and the Bella Moth. The plant is especially high in protein making it a favorite choice of many kinds of mammalian wildlife including deer, rabbits, and muskrat. It can also be used in pastures as livestock forage. The seeds are eaten by gamebirds and songbirds, including the Mourning Dove, Bobwhite, Turkey, and Junco.”
> 
> --Prairie Moon Nursery
