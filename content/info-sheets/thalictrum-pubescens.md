## _Thalictrum pubescens_

**Native to:** All of New England

**Life cycle:** Perennial

**Light**: Full/Partial Sun

**Soil Moisture:** Medium to Wet

**Height**: 18-60"<br />**Spacing**: 18-36"

**Blooms**: Summer<br />
**Bloom Color:** White

**Natural Habitat:** Human-disturbed or -maintained habitats, river or stream floodplains, forests, marshes, meadows and fields, shores of rivers or lakes, swamps, wetland margins

**Benefits**: Attracts Butterflies, Attracts Bees, Host Plant (eg. Canadian Owlet Moth, Pink-Patched Looper Moth), Deer/Rabbit Resistant, Low Maintenance

> "Best planted in sunny, moist sites, tall meadow rue can reach heights of up to ten feet, though perhaps infrequently. With small fluffy flowers followed by white to pink fruits borne by the female plants, this species is an important pollen and nectar source for a variety of insects"
> 
> -- Native Plant Trust
