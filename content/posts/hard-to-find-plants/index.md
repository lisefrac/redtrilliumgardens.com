## Hard to find plants available now/soon

I wanted to let you know we now have (or will soon have) some hard-to-source plants available for sale! These are plants you don't see a lot in the nursery trade because they are hard to grow from seed, or very slow growing. But we are thrilled to bring them to you!

**Teaberry/Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens).** This plant with evergreen foliage and red berries *is* the one you're thinking of -- the one with the titular minty smell! (As opposed to the many other species with the common name "wintergreen"!) It is especially difficult to grow from seed, as it requires unique conditions (bottom heat, 100% atmospheric moisture, a moss substrate, rainwater only) and grows very slowly. These seeds were started last year, and the plants I have available are in pint pots, and aren't more than 2-3" tall. 

Given the amount of time that goes into growing these, $8 for one of these is an incredible bargain. I'm honestly growing them more as an experiment in whether or not I can; profit is secondary!

These plants demand an acid soil and should only be watered with rainwater or distilled water, as they are very sensitive to dissolved minerals in tap water. However, once sited in a shady area with acid soil, they slowly spread by runners (and eventually, seed), forming a beautiful evergreen groundcover.

We will have a supply of these available within the next two weeks, so we encourage you to reserve yours now! 

**Partridgeberry (Mitchella repens).** Commonly mistaken for wintergreen, this is an even *smaller* evergreen groundcover with red berries! (But once you know both species, you'll never mistake them). While it isn't as fussy about conditions as wintergreen, it is a slow grower.
